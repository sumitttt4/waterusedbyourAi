Can you explain how you'd estimate the water footprint of an AI prompt, end to end?
Sure! You can't measure it directly, so you model it: count the tokens, convert tokens to energy using a per-model Watt-hour figure, then convert energy to water using the datacenter's cooling water plus the water used to generate the electricity. Every step is a public estimate with wide error bars, and the honest per-prompt number turns out to be a fraction of a millilitre — not the litres the memes suggest.
Wait, so the viral "46 litres" meme is wrong? By how much?
Off by about 5–6 orders of magnitude. Google published that a median Gemini text prompt uses roughly 0.26 mL of water. OpenAI's Sam Altman cited about 0.32 mL per ChatGPT query. The real story is aggregate scale — billions of queries add up — but any single "Hello" costs a fraction of a droplet, not a bathtub. The meme is funny, but the numbers behind it are… not real.
